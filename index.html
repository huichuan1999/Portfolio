Huichuan Wang (RIVER)

Visual Artist, Illustrator, Designer, Computational Artist

In summary, my research direction and focus revolve around the simulation of living organisms, drawing inspiration from biological structures and processes. By combining elements of computer science, mathematics, and art, I aim to explore the intricacies of life, self-organization, and artificial intervention in a virtual environment. This involves studying and replicating the morphology of ancient life forms, such as the Ediacaran biota, and applying concepts like fractals, L-systems, and genetic algorithms to simulate their growth patterns and developmental processes. Through this interdisciplinary approach, I seek to gain a deeper understanding of the nature of life and the implications of human intervention in shaping its evolution.
